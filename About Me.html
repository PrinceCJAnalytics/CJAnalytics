About Me

Prince Onochie Chukwuma James (Aka PrinceCJ) is a dedicated and accomplished professional with a strong foundation in Data Analytics & Science, Probability Distributions, and Artificial Intelligence (AI). His research interests include applications in Survival Analysis, Competing Risks, Acceptance Sampling, Optimum Sampling, and Mask data.

Prince attained a B.Sc in Statistics from Nnamdi Azikiwe University (2020-2024). His academic excellence is exemplified by receiving the Merit Award for Overall Best Graduand in the Statistics Department at Nnamdi Azikiwe University in 2024.

Throughout his academic career, Prince has demonstrated significant leadership qualities. He served as the Class Representative for the class of 2024 in the Department of Statistics (2022-2024), President of the Department of Statistics (2023-2024), and President of the Data Science Club within the Department of Statistics (2023-2024). He have also been an active member of professional bodies such as the National Association of Statistics Students (NASS) since 2020, the Council of Class Representatives (CCR) at Nnamdi Azikiwe University (2022-2024), and the Council of Departmental Presidents at Nnamdi Azikiwe University (2024).

Prince professional experience includes roles as a Sales Representative at See Hope Global Service (May 2017 - March 2018) and a Class Teacher for Primary 5-6 at Kings & Priests, Nursery & Primary School (March 2018 - August 2019). Since 2022, he has been a Tutor for Statistical & Mathematical Courses in the Statistics Department and beyond at Nnamdi Azikiwe University, Awka, Nigeria. He has also been working as a self-employed Data Scientist & Analyst since 2023

He possesses excellent written and verbal communication skills in English. His technical expertise includes proficiency in programming languages such as Python, R, and SQL, as well as analytical tools like Power BI, Excel, Minitab, SPSS, and Tableau. He is also skilled in document preparation tools such as Microsoft Word and LaTeX. His broader competencies span academic research, data analysis, and science-related services, including teaching and training.

Prince has contributed to the academic community through research publications, including but a few "A new reduced quantile function for generating families of distributions" in Annals of Mathematics and Physics (2024) and "A novel extension of rayleigh distribution: Characterization, estimation, simulations and applications" in the Journal of Xidian University (2024). He has also presented his research at conferences and holds a Credle Badge for the Applied Data Science Lab from World Quant University, USA.

Statistical Coding Software Expertise

Python

My proficiency in Python is central to my data science and analytics services. I leverage its powerful libraries like Pandas for efficient data manipulation, cleaning, and preprocessing, and NumPy for complex numerical computations. For developing predictive models and implementing machine learning solutions, particularly in areas aligning with my research interests in AI, I utilize Scikit-learn and other relevant packages. In my services, this translates to building custom solutions for data-driven decision-making, from exploratory data analysis to deploying machine learning applications.

In Training: I introduce Python as a foundational language for aspiring data scientists and analysts. My courses cover everything from basic syntax to advanced applications in data manipulation with Pandas, visualization with Matplotlib and Seaborn, and an introduction to machine learning concepts, ensuring learners gain practical, hands-on experience.

R

With a strong background in statistical analysis, R is a key tool in my arsenal. I use R for advanced statistical modeling, in-depth data exploration, and creating high-quality, publication-ready visualizations, particularly for projects requiring rigorous statistical backing. Its extensive package ecosystem, including the Tidyverse, allows me to conduct nuanced data analysis and support complex research, including areas like probability distributions and competing risks analysis.

In Training: When teaching R, I focus on its strengths in statistical computing and data visualization. I guide learners through using RStudio, understanding data structures, and applying statistical tests and models. My training emphasizes reproducible research using R Markdown, enabling students to produce comprehensive analytical reports.

SQL

My expertise in SQL allows me to efficiently manage, query, and manipulate data stored in relational databases. In my data analytics services, I use SQL to extract, filter, aggregate, and prepare large datasets for detailed analysis or for use in other analytical tools. This is crucial for tasks ranging from customer segmentation to performance metric tracking, ensuring data integrity and efficient data retrieval.

In Training: I teach SQL as an essential skill for anyone working with data. My training covers writing effective queries to retrieve and manipulate data, understanding database structures, and performing complex operations like JOINs and aggregations, equipping learners with the ability to interact confidently with databases.

Statistical Computational Software Expertise

Power BI

I utilize Power BI to transform complex datasets into clear, interactive, and actionable dashboards and reports. My services include developing custom BI solutions that help clients visualize their data, track key performance indicators, and gain insights for better decision-making. I focus on creating intuitive and impactful visualizations that tell a compelling story with data.

In Training: In my Power BI training sessions, I empower users to connect to various data sources, perform data cleaning and transformation using Power Query, model data, and create dynamic reports and dashboards. The emphasis is on practical application, enabling participants to quickly leverage Power BI for their own analytical needs.

Excel

While adept with more advanced tools, I recognize the continued importance and versatility of Excel in data analysis. I use Excel for quick data exploration, cleaning, creating pivot tables for data summarization, and developing straightforward visualizations. It's often a practical tool for initial data handling and for clients who primarily operate within the Microsoft Office ecosystem.

In Training: My Excel training for data analysis covers essential functions, data cleaning techniques, creating effective charts, and mastering PivotTables. I aim to help learners maximize Excel's capabilities for everyday data tasks and build a solid foundation for more advanced analytics.

Minitab

My experience with Minitab is particularly relevant for projects requiring robust statistical analysis, especially in areas like quality control and process improvement, which can overlap with my interest in optimum sampling. I use Minitab for hypothesis testing, regression analysis, and Design of Experiments (DOE).

In Training: When incorporating Minitab into teaching, I focus on its application in statistical problem-solving. Students learn to navigate its interface, perform various statistical tests, and interpret the output, making it accessible for those needing to apply statistical methods in fields like engineering or quality assurance.

SPSS

With my background in statistical education and services, SPSS is another tool I am proficient in, particularly for quantitative data analysis common in survey research and social sciences. I utilize SPSS for tasks such as descriptive statistics, bivariate and multivariate analysis, and predictive modeling for specific research questions.

In Training: I teach SPSS by focusing on its user-friendly interface for conducting complex statistical analyses. My training covers data entry and management, performing a wide range of statistical tests, and interpreting the results, which is invaluable for students and professionals in research-oriented fields.

Tableau

I leverage Tableau to create sophisticated and highly interactive data visualizations and dashboards. My services involve using Tableau to help clients explore their data dynamically, uncover hidden patterns, and communicate complex information in an accessible visual format. This is key to delivering impactful data stories and enabling insightful business intelligence.

In Training: My Tableau training focuses on equipping individuals with the skills to connect to data, design compelling visualizations, and build interactive dashboards. Participants learn to use Tableau's drag-and-drop interface effectively, create calculated fields, and share their insights, transforming them into proficient data storytellers.
